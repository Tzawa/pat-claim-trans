1. A nitride crystal substrate configured as a self-standing substrate composed of a single crystal of a Group III nitride semiconductor, the substrate having a main surface whose closest low-index crystal plane is a (0001) surface, wherein
	X-ray rocking curve measurement of a (0002) surface is performed at each position on a straight line passing through the center within the main surface;
	a peak angle ω, which is an angle formed between X-rays incident on the main surface and the main surface and at which diffraction intensity becomes maximum, is plotted with respect to position on the straight line;
	when the peak angle ω is approximated by a linear function of the position, an error of the peak angle ω measured at each position on the straight line with respect to the linear function is 0.05° or less,
	the nitride crystal substrate has a plurality of threading dislocations propagating in a thickness direction, and
	a set of the plurality of threading dislocations includes a plurality of curved dislocations having curved portions in which an inclination of dislocation lines with respect to a normal to the main surface continuously changes.
